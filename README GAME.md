# Wealth Manager Arena — Game / Product README

## Overview
Wealth Manager Arena: The Investing Game is a gamified investment learning experience for beginners.

Players build and manage a portfolio using a simplified but realistic set of asset classes. They then progress through accelerated market time, react to news, survive crises, and learn the long-term effects of their decisions.

The product is designed to teach core investing principles through simulation, feedback, and replayability.

---

## Core Learning Goals

The game teaches players:
- risk profiling
- diversification
- long-term investing
- asset allocation
- how volatility affects portfolios
- how to react to market news
- why disciplined investing beats emotional decision-making

The game should discourage gambling behavior and reward thoughtful, risk-aware investing.

---

## Main Modes

### 1. Sandbox Mode
A solo self-learning mode designed for overall understanding and experimentation.

Features:
- choose one of three AI assistance levels
- choose which markets / asset classes are enabled
- build a portfolio from available assets
- progress through time-accelerated market scenarios
- receive educational summaries and performance feedback

Purpose:
- safe exploration
- beginner practice
- understanding how portfolios behave over time

---

### 2. Competitive Mode
A multiplayer mode where users must make decisions under time pressure.

In this mode, each tick gives players a limited amount of time to validate their decisions.

#### A. Battle Mode
- one-vs-one or team-vs-team format
- choose markets to include
- choose AI level
- same scenario for all players
- leaderboard-based outcome

#### B. Ranked Mode
- most realistic game mode
- all markets enabled
- leaderboard / rating system similar to chess ELO or trading score
- only AI Level 3 available
- designed to reward skill, discipline, and consistency

Purpose:
- competition
- event use
- replayability
- skill progression

---

## AI Assistance Levels

### AI Level 1 — Coach Mode
Best AI support for beginners.

Features:
- predictive hints
- guided suggestions
- educational explanations
- shows similar real-life cases when news/crashes happen
- teaches how to react to events

Use case:
- first-time users
- financial literacy workshops
- beginner onboarding

---

### AI Level 2 — Analyst Mode
Intermediate support.

Features:
- limited predictive information
- gives context and some real-life comparisons
- less direct help than Coach Mode
- helps users interpret events without fully guiding decisions

Use case:
- users who already understand basics
- transition from guided learning to independent decision-making

---

### AI Level 3 — Real Market Mode
Most realistic and least assisted mode.

Features:
- no predictive information
- only news is shown
- no hints
- no direct comparison guidance
- users decide alone

Use case:
- ranked play
- advanced learning
- realistic decision pressure

---

## Markets / Asset Classes

Players can choose which markets to include through checkboxes or selection controls.

Supported market categories:
- equity indices (example: SMI, Euro Stoxx 50, Dow Jones)
- selected individual stocks
- bonds
- currencies (example: USD/CHF, EUR/CHF)
- gold / commodities
- crypto (BTC and ETH)

This lets the game stay flexible for both simple beginner setups and more advanced scenarios.

---

## Time System

The game uses accelerated time so players can experience long-term investing quickly.

### Two time modes

#### 1. Monthly Mode
- one tick = one month
- stops at all impactful news
- more detailed learning mode
- useful for understanding gradual portfolio evolution

#### 2. Yearly Mode
- one tick = one year
- stops only at major news
- faster strategic overview mode
- useful for demonstrating long-term investing outcomes quickly

---

## News System

News is a core mechanic. It creates decision points and teaches players to distinguish signal from noise.

### 1. Major News
- highest importance
- examples: war, financial crashes, major systemic events
- strongly affects markets
- real-life similar scenarios can be shown

### 2. Impactful News
- medium importance
- relevant market-moving events
- can influence one or more asset classes
- real-life similar scenarios can be shown

### 3. Ordinary News
- lowest importance
- daily or smaller events
- useful for teaching players not to overreact
- usually no real-life similar case shown

Purpose of the news system:
- teach interpretation
- test emotional discipline
- create varied scenarios
- reinforce long-term thinking

## Measures and Scoring

The game should not judge users only by profit.
It should evaluate how responsibly and intelligently they invested.

### Core measures

#### 1. Total Return
How much the portfolio gained or lost overall.

#### 2. Volatility
How much the portfolio value fluctuated over time.

#### 3. Maximum Drawdown
The largest drop from peak to lowest point.

#### 4. Diversification Score
How concentrated or balanced the portfolio is across asset classes.

#### 5. Risk-Match Score
How well the portfolio matches the player’s intended risk profile.

#### 6. Long-Term Discipline Score
Measures whether the player stayed consistent or panicked too often.

#### 7. Reaction Quality to News
Evaluates whether the player responded well, overreacted, or underreacted to events.

#### 8. Timing Penalty / Overtrading Score
Tracks whether the player traded too frequently or too aggressively.

#### 9. Resilience Score
Measures how well the portfolio survived shocks relative to benchmark portfolios.

#### 10. Benchmark Comparison
Compares the player to reference strategies such as:
- cash only
- balanced portfolio
- stock-heavy portfolio
- ideal beginner portfolio

## Summary and Learning Feedback

A major part of the game is the feedback layer.

### 1. End-of-Tick Summary
After each month or year:
- what changed in markets
- what happened to the portfolio
- what the player decided
- whether risk/return balance improved or worsened

### 2. Move Review
For each important decision:
- what the player did
- why it helped or hurt
- a safer alternative
- a more aggressive alternative
- what a diversified investor might have done

### 3. Counterfactual Simulator
Shows better alternatives without oversimplifying learning:
- best return scenario
- best risk-adjusted scenario
- safest scenario
- most realistic beginner scenario

### 4. Portfolio Health Dashboard
At the end of a run, show:
- return
- volatility
- drawdown
- diversification score
- concentration level
- benchmark ranking
- AI assistance used
- market conditions faced

### 5. “What You Learned” Summary
Generate 3–5 personalized takeaways such as:
- you were too concentrated
- you overreacted to short-term news
- diversification improved your performance
- your risk level did not match your stated profile
- you handled downturns well but missed upside

### 6. Crisis Recap
Show:
- which crises/events happened
- how they affected markets
- how the player reacted
- how similar real-life cases behaved

### 7. Behavior Profile
Classify the player with a simple label such as:
- cautious allocator
- panic seller
- overconfident trader
- disciplined investor
- diversified planner

### 8. Improvement Mission
At the end of each run, give a replay challenge:
- keep max 20% in crypto
- survive the next crash with lower drawdown
- beat the benchmark with fewer trades

## UX Principles

The UI should be:
- simple for beginners
- visually clear
- chart-driven but not overwhelming
- educational without feeling academic
- playful without feeling childish
- intuitive in both solo and event environments

Important UI priorities:
- quick onboarding
- clear asset selection
- simple allocation flow
- easy-to-understand feedback
- strong use of charts and visual comparison

---

## Technical Direction

Suggested product structure:
- frontend for gameplay and visualization
- lightweight backend for session state, scoring, and leaderboards
- optional real-time layer for multiplayer / battle mode
- support for historical or synthetic financial data
- optional AI/LLM layer for summaries, hints, and explanations

The MVP should prioritize:
1. a playable sandbox mode
2. at least one market phase change
3. a working scoring system
4. an educational summary function
5. basic competitive/event-readiness if time allows

---

## Product Positioning

Wealth Manager Arena is not a high-frequency trading simulator.
It is not a gambling product.
It is not built for expert traders.

It is a beginner-first investing education game designed to build confidence, understanding, and better long-term decision-making.

---

## Final Product Goal

The goal of the product is to let beginners experience investing rather than only read about it.

By combining:
- portfolio creation
- accelerated time
- realistic market events
- competitive play
- personalized learning feedback

the game helps users understand investing in a practical, engaging, and safe way.
